Three Phase Full Wave Silicon Controlled Rectifier (SCR)

Post Test

Q1. A three phase full controlled converter is feeding the armature of a separately excited DC motor. The motor has to also operate in quadrant - III. Which of the following method is suitable?

a. By connecting a free wheeling diode across the armature in addition to adjusting the triggering angle α

b. By adjusting the triggering angle α only

c. By adjusting the triggering angle α followed by armature connection reversal

d. By operating the triggering angle α > π/2 rad

Q2. For what firing angle does a 3-Φ full wave bridge rectifier act as a natural commutated inverter?

a. α>90°

b. α<90°

c. α>180°

d. α<180°

Q3. Three phase full wave converter is supplying purely resistive load at 300 V dc for 0° firing angle. What would be the output voltage for 90° firing angle?

a. 0 V

b. 40 V

c. 50 V

d. 100 V

Q4. What is the average output voltage of three phase full wave controlled rectifier with α<π/3?

a. `(3√3)/π` `V_(mp)` cos⁡α

b. `(√3)/π` `V_(mp)` cos⁡α

c. `(3√3)/π` `V_(mp)` cos2⁡α

d. `(√3)/π` `V_(mp)` cos2⁡α

Q5. A three-phase controlled rectifier has an input voltage which is 480 V rms at 60 Hz. The load is modeled as a series resistance and inductance with R = 10 Ω and L = 50 mH. Determine the delay angle required to produce an average current of 50 A in the load?

a. 50°

b. 39.5°

c. 27.25°

d. 20°
