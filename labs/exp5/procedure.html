To determine the Ferrous content in the supplied sample of iron ore by titrimetric analysis against standard K 2 Cr 2 O 7 solution using K 3 Fe(CN) 6 as external indicator.

Procedure

Procedure of Removal of Hardness of Water by Ion Exchange Column method

Follow these steps to determine the Removal of Hardness of Water by Ion Exchange Column

  1. Setup: Connect the ion exchange column to the water source following the manufacturer’s guidelines.
    Ensure proper plumbing and control valves are in place for regulating water flow.
  2. Preparation of Resin:Follow the manufacturer’s instructions to prepare the ion exchange resin.
    Fill the ion exchange column with the prepared resin, ensuring uniform packing.
  3. Calibration: Regenerate the resin if necessary as per the manufacturer’s guidelines.
    Calibrate the column to ensure optimal performance.
  4. Water Sample Collection: Collect a representative water sample to test for hardness.
  5. Column Operation: Pass the water sample through the ion exchange column.
    Monitor the effluent for changes in hardness.
  6. Analysis: Periodically test the effluent for hardness using appropriate methods (e.g., titration).
    Record the results.
  7. Regeneration (if necessary):When the resin reaches its capacity, initiate the regeneration process.
    Prepare a brine solution according to the manufacturer’s recommendations.
    Pass the brine solution through the column to regenerate the resin.
  8. Monitoring and Record Keeping: Continuously monitor the column operation, adjusting flow rates if necessary.
    Record data, including inlet and outlet hardness levels, flow rates, and any abnormalities observed during the experiment.
  9. Results and Conclusion: Analyze the collected data to evaluate the efficiency of the ion exchange column in removing water hardness.
    Draw conclusions based on the results, discussing any observed trends or variations.

Safety Precautions: Wear safety goggles and a lab coat to protect against potential splashes. Handle chemicals with care and follow proper laboratory safety protocols.

Clean-Up: Dispose of waste solutions properly. Clean and return all glassware to its designated place.
