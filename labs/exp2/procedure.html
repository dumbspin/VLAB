Determination of temporary and permanent hardness of water by complexometric titration.

Procedure

Procedure of determination of temporary and permanent hardness of water by complexometric titration.

Follow these steps to determine the temporary and permanent hardness of water by complexometric titration.

  1. Setup:
    a. Connect the ion exchange column to the water source following the manufacturer's guidelines. Ensure proper plumbing and control valves for water flow regulation.
    b. Prepare the ion exchange resin according to the manufacturer's instructions. Fill the ion exchange column with the resin, ensuring uniform packing.
  2. Calibration:
    a. Regenerate the resin if necessary following the manufacturer's guidelines.
    b. Calibrate the ion exchange column to ensure accurate and consistent performance.
  3. Water sample collection: Collect a representative water sample for hardness testing.
  4. Temporary Hardness Determination:
    a. Pass the water sample through the ion exchange column. Monitor the effluent for changes in hardness.
    b. Titrate the effluent with a standardized EDTA solution using a suitable indicator (e.g., Eriochrome Black T) to determine the volume required for complexation. Record the volume.
  5. Precipitation of Permanent Hardness:
    a. Add a known volume of sodium oxalate solution to the remaining water sample to precipitate permanent hardness.
    b. Filter the solution to separate the precipitate.
  6. Permanent Hardness Determination: Titrate the filtered solution with the same standardized EDTA solution, using the same indicator. Record the volume required for complexation.
  7. Calculations:
    a. Calculate the temporary hardness from the volume of EDTA used in the first titration.
    b. Calculate the permanent hardness from the difference between the volumes of EDTA used in the two titrations.
    c. Calculate the total hardness by summing the temporary and permanent hardness values.
  8. Monitoring and Record Keeping: Continuously monitor the ion exchange column operation, adjusting flow rates if necessary. Record data, including inlet and outlet hardness levels, flow rates, and any abnormalities observed during the experiment.
  9. Results and Conclusion:
    a. Analyze the collected data to evaluate the efficiency of the ion exchange column in removing water hardness.
    b. Draw conclusions based on the results, discussing any observed trends or variations. Consider the overall effectiveness of the complexometric titration in determining water hardness.
    Ensure safety precautions, accurate measurements, and adherence to standard procedures throughout the experiment. Adjust volumes and concentrations based on the specific characteristics of the water sample and reagents used.
